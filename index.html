One guided planning arc

Earlier choices carry forward automatically, while plain-language checks surface questions a teacher may revise or knowingly override.

1 · Purpose and source

Start with why the message matters

Name the learner context and communication outcome, then record a source’s creator, region, access supports, and reuse limits.

2 · Input and communication

Process meaning, then use it

Plan interpretive or structured input before learners exchange information or create for a real audience.

3 · Evidence and revision

Look for what learners show

Write editable, observable achievement descriptors and keep behavior or effort separate. Plan feedback and fresh evidence.
