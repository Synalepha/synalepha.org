About

Men's mental health is in crisis. Suicide rates are 4× higher than women's. 15% of young men have no close friends. 40% have never spoken to anyone about their mental health. The loneliness epidemic isn't abstract — it's real, and it's killing men quietly.

We believe the answer isn't more clinical therapy (though that has its place). The answer is community. Real conversations. Real presence. Men sitting together, being honest about what's hard, and finding strength in shared experience.

This isn't a support group run by a therapist. It's a group of men supporting men — because sometimes what you need isn't a professional, it's a brother.

The data is clear: men are isolated, depressed, and silently dying. Traditional therapy helps — but it's not the whole answer. What men need most is connection. A space where they can be honest without judgment. Where they don't have to "perform" masculinity.

The male loneliness epidemic is driven by structural forces — the "man box," disappearing third places, economic pressure, emotional repression — but the solution is simple: men sitting together, talking honestly, and being there for each other.

How It Works
