Member Guide

Not therapy. Just community.

A guide for new members. What to expect, ground rules, and how to participate.

Welcome

Straight truth

If you're reading this, you're probably wondering what to expect. Here's the straight truth: this is not therapy. There's no clinician, no diagnosis, no homework. This is a group of men meeting to talk honestly, listen to each other, and support each other through the stuff that's hard.

You don't need to be in crisis. You don't need a label. You just need to show up.

First Meeting

What to expect

Six things that make Synalepha different.

  • Weekly meetings, about 2 hours
  • Start with a quick check-in — "how's everyone doing?"
  • Then the conversation flows. You speak when you're ready.
  • No agenda. No required sharing.
  • End with a check-out and next meeting date
  • Usually 8–12 people
  • New members always welcome — open group

The Agreement

Ground rules

Six principles that keep this space safe and real.

1 Confidentiality

What's said in the group stays in the group. No gossip.

2 No Cross-Talk

When someone speaks, let them finish. Listen first.

3 No Advice (Unless Asked)

Most men need to be heard, not fixed.

4 It's Okay to Sit Out

You don't have to talk. Listening is enough.

5 Respect

Be kind. Be patient. No judgment.

6 What's Shared Stays Here

Don't repeat what you hear outside the room.

Your Role

How to participate

There's no wrong way to show up.

  • You don't need to prepare anything
  • You can share when you're ready — no pressure
  • You can just listen — that's a valid contribution
  • You can leave early if you need to
  • Try to attend regularly — consistency builds trust
  • If you're struggling between meetings, reach out
