Three lesson sparks

Novice · Interpretive

A neighborhood choice

Compare two short menus or shop notices. Learners identify which option fits three people’s stated preferences, then say what they would choose.

Intermediate · Interpersonal

One word, several places

Notice regional vocabulary in authentic posts or clips, infer meaning from context, and ask a partner which terms they use or recognize.

Intermediate · Presentational

Community audio guide

Create a one-minute recommendation for a place, using a model from a relevant Spanish-speaking community.

Regional and inclusive-language notes

Teach variation as information

Label where a source comes from and let learners notice pronunciation, vocabulary, and forms such as vos, tú, and usted. Avoid presenting one variety as neutral or universal.

Describe choices, not absolutes

Communities differ in how they discuss gender-inclusive language. Give context for forms learners may encounter, distinguish institutional guidance from community practice, and avoid claiming consensus.

Before this reaches learners

  • Can every learner access the source in a usable format?
  • Does the source context represent a community rather than a language stereotype?
  • Can learners show understanding before they are required to produce extensively?
  • Is there more than one viable way to participate or demonstrate evidence?
